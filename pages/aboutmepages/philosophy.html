Philosophy

Philosophy is the love of wisdom. It invites us to question our beliefs, challenge assumptions, and explore the fundamental nature of existence, knowledge, and values.

Socratic Dialogue

“The unexamined life is not worth living.” – Socrates

In the spirit of the Socratic Method, consider these guiding questions:

  • What does it mean to live a good life?
  • How do we distinguish right from wrong?
  • Can knowledge ever be certain, or does doubt drive discovery?
  • What is the nature of reality beyond our perceptions?

Key Philosophers

  • Socrates – Pioneered critical dialogue and self-examination.
  • Plato – Explored the world of ideal Forms and justice.
  • Aristotle – Systematized ethics, logic, and natural sciences.
  • John Locke – Foundation of modern empiricism and political theory.
  • Immanuel Kant – Bridged reason and morality in his critical philosophy.
